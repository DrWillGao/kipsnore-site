Plenty of people snore with their mouth wide open and wake with a dry mouth. Mouth breathing snoring has a clear cause, and some of the fixes are simple.

## Why does mouth breathing snoring happen?

When you sleep with your mouth open, the lower jaw drops back. That pulls the tongue and soft palate towards the back of your throat and narrows the airway. Air squeezes through a smaller gap and sets the relaxed tissues fluttering, and that flutter is the snore. Lying on your back compounds it. See our guides to [what causes snoring](/blog/what-causes-snoring/) and the [types of snoring](/blog/types-of-snoring/).

## How can I tell if I am a mouth breather at night?

You may not notice it while asleep, but the morning signs are fairly clear:

- A dry or sticky mouth when you wake up
- A sore or scratchy throat first thing
- Morning bad breath despite cleaning your teeth at night
- Waking with chapped lips
- A partner who reports your mouth hanging open while you snore

A recording can settle it. [Kip](/) scores your snoring on your iPhone overnight, so you can see whether your changes quieten things down. The audio stays on the device.

## Does a blocked nose cause mouth breathing and snoring?

Often, yes. If your nose is congested, you breathe through your mouth instead. Colds, allergies, a deviated septum and smoke-irritated tissue can all narrow the passage. Clearing the nose is the first thing to try: saline rinses, treating allergies, and avoiding cigarette smoke. Our guide to [a blocked nose and snoring](/blog/blocked-nose-snoring/) has the detail.

## Do chin straps and mouth tape work?

These aids aim to keep the mouth closed so you breathe through your nose.

| Aid | Idea | Honest caveat |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Chin strap | Holds the jaw up so the mouth stays shut | Comfort varies; useless if your nose is blocked |
| Mouth tape | Encourages nose breathing | Only sensible if you can breathe freely through your nose; avoid if congested |

Neither aid treats sleep apnoea. If you breathe through your mouth because your nose is blocked, fix the nose first. For more, see [anti-snoring devices that work](/blog/anti-snoring-devices-that-work/).

## When should I see a GP?

Snoring is very common and usually not serious. See your GP if it is affecting your or your partner's sleep, and book an appointment if anyone notices you gasping, choking, or stopping breathing in your sleep. An app cannot diagnose sleep apnoea; only a sleep study can.

## FAQ

### Is mouth taping safe for snoring?

For most people who can breathe easily through their nose, taping the lips lightly is low risk. Avoid it if your nose is congested, if you have a cold, or if you feel breathless. It does not treat sleep apnoea, so see your GP if you gasp or stop breathing.

### Why is my mouth so dry when I wake up?

A dry mouth on waking usually means you slept with your mouth open for hours, which dries the tissues. A blocked nose is the common reason your mouth falls open, so easing congestion often helps.

### Does breathing through your nose stop snoring?

Nose breathing keeps the jaw and tongue in a better position, which can reduce snoring for some people. It is not a guaranteed fix, because snoring also depends on weight, alcohol, position and throat anatomy. If it persists after you clear your nose, raise it with your GP.
