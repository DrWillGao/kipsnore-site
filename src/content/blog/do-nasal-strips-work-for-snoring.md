  <p>Do nasal strips work for snoring? They can help if your snoring starts in the nose, by holding your nostrils open. They do little for snoring from the throat or soft palate. They are not a treatment for sleep apnoea. If snoring worries you, see your GP.</p>

Nasal strips are cheap, drug-free and easy to try, which is why so many people reach for them first. The honest answer is that they help some snorers a lot and others not at all. The trick is working out which group you are in.

## How do nasal strips work?

A nasal strip is a stiff adhesive band you stick across the bridge of your nose. As it tries to straighten, it gently pulls the sides of your nostrils outward, widening the nasal valve. That can lower nasal resistance and let air move more smoothly.

This only matters if the narrow point is in your nose. Snoring is the sound of relaxed tissue vibrating as air pushes through a narrowed airway. When the narrowing sits in the nose, easing it can quieten things. When the soft palate, uvula or tongue base is collapsing at the back of the throat, opening your nostrils does not reach the problem.

## When do nasal strips help, and when do they not?

| More likely to help | Less likely to help |
| --- | --- |
| Blocked or congested nose | Throat or palate snoring |
| Cold, allergies or a narrow nasal valve | Snoring linked to weight or alcohol |
| You breathe better through your nose when awake | Loud snoring with pauses or gasping |

A [blocked nose makes snoring more likely](/blog/blocked-nose-snoring/), so people whose congestion clears in the day are good candidates. If your snoring is positional or throat-based, a strip is the wrong tool. See [what causes snoring](/blog/what-causes-snoring/) for the full picture, and [anti-snoring devices that work](/blog/anti-snoring-devices-that-work/) for stronger options.

## How can I tell if they are actually working?

Memory is a poor judge of a quiet night. The cleaner approach is to measure.

1. Record three normal nights with no strip.
2. Then record three nights wearing one.
3. Compare the loudness, frequency and trend, not just how you felt.

An app like [Kip](/) records and scores your snoring on your iPhone, with the audio never leaving the device. Tracking the change is the only honest way to know if a strip earns its place. More on the method in [how to track snoring](/blog/how-to-track-snoring/).

One caution: loud snoring with breathing pauses or gasping is not something a strip should manage. That pattern is consistent with disrupted sleep and is worth raising with your GP.

## FAQ

### Do nasal strips work for everyone who snores?

No. They mainly help snoring that starts in the nose, such as congestion or a narrow nasal valve. If your snoring comes from the throat or soft palate relaxing, strips usually make little difference.

### Can nasal strips treat sleep apnoea?

No. Nasal strips are a comfort aid for nasal airflow, not a treatment for sleep apnoea. Only a sleep study can diagnose sleep apnoea. If you have loud snoring with pauses, gasping or daytime sleepiness, see your GP.

### How long should I try nasal strips before giving up?

Give them a fair test of about a week, ideally with tracking on a few nights with and without. If loudness and frequency barely change, your snoring is probably not nasal, and another approach is more likely to help.
