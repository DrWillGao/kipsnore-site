If a partner has nudged you about loud snoring, or you wake up tired no matter how long you slept, it is worth understanding the bigger picture. The topic of sleep apnoea and men's health gets attention because researchers keep finding links between untreated obstructive sleep apnoea (OSA) and things that matter as you get older. None of this is cause to panic. It is a reason for a calm chat with your GP.

## How is sleep apnoea linked to men's health?

OSA is a condition where the airway narrows or closes repeatedly during sleep, so breathing keeps stopping and starting. The NHS notes that, left untreated, it is associated with high blood pressure, stroke, type 2 diabetes, heart disease, low mood and a higher accident risk from tiredness. These are patterns seen across groups of people, not a guarantee for any one person.

| Area | What studies have observed |
| --- | --- |
| Blood pressure | Linked to higher blood pressure |
| Heart | Associated with heart disease and irregular heart rhythms |
| Diabetes | Linked to type 2 diabetes |
| Energy and mood | Daytime sleepiness, low mood and poor concentration |
| Driving | Sleepiness at the wheel raises crash risk |
| Libido | Linked to lower testosterone and erectile difficulties |

## Can sleep apnoea affect the heart and blood pressure?

The British Heart Foundation explains that OSA has been associated with raised blood pressure and heart problems, including atrial fibrillation, although causation is not fully proven and shared factors like weight play a part. If you already watch your [blood pressure](/blog/snoring-and-high-blood-pressure/) or [heart health](/blog/snoring-and-heart-disease/), it is one more reason to take loud snoring seriously.

## Can sleep apnoea affect libido?

Yes, and there is no need to feel awkward about it. A 2022 review found erectile difficulties are common in men with OSA, and a meta-analysis of 18 studies in 1,823 men reported an inverse link between OSA and testosterone, strongest in severe cases. Broken, low-oxygen sleep is thought to be part of the picture. These are associations, so the step is the same: talk to your GP.

## What should I do next?

Take a simple, unhurried approach:

- Notice the pattern: loud snoring, gasping or choking sounds, [tiredness despite a full night](/blog/tired-after-full-night-sleep/).
- Gather a little evidence. Logging your snoring with [Kip](/) gives your GP something concrete to look at.
- See your GP, who may refer you to a sleep clinic. Only a sleep study can diagnose OSA. The [STOP-BANG questionnaire](/blog/stop-bang-questionnaire/) is a common screening start.

## FAQ

### Does snoring mean I have sleep apnoea?

No. Plenty of people snore without having OSA. The thing to watch for alongside loud snoring is breathing that stops and starts, gasping or choking sounds, and feeling very tired in the day. If that sounds familiar, see your GP.

### Can an app diagnose sleep apnoea?

No. An app cannot diagnose sleep apnoea, and neither can a snore tracker. Only a sleep study arranged through your GP or a sleep clinic can diagnose it. Tracking your snoring can still help you describe the pattern clearly to a doctor.

### Should I be worried about my libido?

There is no need to worry, but it is worth raising. Lower testosterone and erectile difficulties have been observed more often in men with OSA, and these are associations rather than certainties. Your GP can look at the whole picture, including your sleep.
