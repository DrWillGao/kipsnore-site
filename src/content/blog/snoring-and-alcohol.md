If a few drinks turns your normal snoring into something that wakes the whole house, you are not imagining it. The link between alcohol and snoring is one of the most reliable ways to make snoring worse on any given night. The good news is that it is also one of the easiest factors to test and change.

## How does alcohol make you snore?

When you are awake, the muscles in your throat hold the airway open. During sleep they relax, the airway narrows, and air moving past the soft tissues makes them vibrate. That vibration is the sound of snoring.

Alcohol is a muscle relaxant. It deepens that natural relaxation, so the soft palate, tongue and throat walls collapse inward more than usual. A narrower airway means faster, more turbulent airflow, which makes the tissues flutter harder and the sound louder. It also blunts the reflex that would normally wake you a little when breathing gets tight, so the airway stays narrowed for longer. The NHS lists drinking too much alcohol among the things that make snoring more likely. You can read more about the [causes of snoring](/blog/what-causes-snoring/) and the [main risk factors](/blog/snoring-risk-factors/).

## What does the evidence say?

A systematic review and meta-analysis pulled together 14 randomised trials covering 422 people. The findings were consistent.

| What was measured | Effect of alcohol |
| --- | --- |
| Apnoea-hypopnoea index (breathing pauses per hour) | Rose by about 2.3 events on average |
| Lowest blood-oxygen level overnight | Fell measurably |
| People who already had sleep apnoea | Larger rise, around 7 extra events an hour |
| People who snore | Around 4 extra events an hour |

In plain terms, alcohol makes snoring louder and breathing more disrupted, and the effect is biggest in the people who already have a problem. That is the opposite of what most people expect from a nightcap.

## When should I stop drinking before bed?

There is no single magic number, but the practical aim is to let most of the alcohol clear your system before you fall asleep. A sensible rule of thumb is to finish your last drink around three to four hours before bed, and to keep the amount modest. A large dose close to lights-out is the worst combination, because the muscle-relaxing effect peaks just as your throat is already relaxing for sleep.

A simple way to check whether drink is your trigger:

1. Track a few normal, alcohol-free nights first.
2. Note how loud and how frequent your snoring is.
3. Compare those to nights after a couple of drinks.

Tracking with an app like [Kip](/) records and scores your snoring on your iPhone, so you can see whether cutting back actually changes the numbers rather than relying on a half-asleep partner's impression. If you want broader ideas, see [how to stop snoring naturally](/blog/stop-snoring-naturally/).

## When to see your GP

Cutting alcohol is worth trying, but it is not a fix for everything. If you snore loudly most nights, your partner has noticed you stop breathing, gasp or choke in your sleep, or you wake up exhausted no matter how long you were in bed, talk to your GP. Those patterns can be consistent with sleep apnoea, which only a clinical sleep study can diagnose. Kip is a wellness and screening tool, not a medical device, and it cannot diagnose anything. ## FAQ

### Does alcohol cause sleep apnoea?

Alcohol does not cause sleep apnoea on its own, but it makes it worse on the nights you drink by relaxing the airway and lowering blood oxygen. The evidence shows a larger effect in people who already have the condition. If you suspect apnoea, see your GP, as only a clinical sleep study can diagnose it.

### Will quitting alcohol stop my snoring?

It might reduce it, especially if drink is your main trigger, but snoring has several causes including weight, nasal congestion and sleeping position. Cutting alcohol is a sensible first step. Tracking a few nights with and without a drink is the easiest way to see how much difference it makes for you.

### How long before bed should I stop drinking?

Aim to finish your last drink around three to four hours before bed and keep the amount modest. That gives your body time to clear most of the alcohol before sleep, so it is not relaxing your throat at the same time as your muscles naturally relax for the night.
