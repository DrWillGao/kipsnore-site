If you have never been a snorer and suddenly are, something has changed. The good news is that the cause is often obvious once you look for it, and many triggers are easy to undo. So why am I snoring all of a sudden? Here is what usually lies behind a sudden start.

## Why am I snoring all of a sudden?

Snoring happens when air passes through a narrowed airway and makes the soft tissue at the back of your throat and nose vibrate. Anything that narrows that airway, or relaxes the muscles holding it open, can switch on snoring overnight. The most common new triggers are:

| Trigger | Why it starts snoring |
| --- | --- |
| Cold, allergies or a blocked nose | Congestion forces faster airflow through a narrowed nose |
| Recent weight gain | Extra tissue around the neck crowds the airway |
| Alcohol before bed | Relaxes the throat muscles that hold the airway open |
| New sedative or sleeping pill | Same airway-relaxing effect as alcohol |
| Sleeping on your back | Gravity pulls the tongue and soft palate backwards |
| Smoking | Inflames and narrows the airway |

A blocked nose is one of the most common culprits. People with nasal congestion are roughly three times more likely to snore. For more on this, see our guide to [a blocked nose and snoring](/blog/blocked-nose-snoring/).

## Will it go away on its own?

Often, yes. If a cold or hay fever started it, the snoring usually fades as the congestion clears. If alcohol or a late nightcap is the trigger, a few dry nights will tell you. The slower-burning causes, like [weight gain](/blog/snoring-and-weight/), take longer to reverse but respond well to small changes. The tricky part is knowing which trigger is yours. Tracking your snoring with an app like [Kip](/) for a week or two, while you change one thing at a time, shows you whether a change actually helped. For the full picture of triggers, see [what causes snoring](/blog/what-causes-snoring/).

## When should new snoring see a GP?

Most new snoring is not serious. Raise it with your GP if it comes with breathing pauses, gasping or choking sounds, or strong daytime tiredness. New snoring during pregnancy is also worth mentioning to your midwife or GP, since it can be linked to raised blood pressure. An app cannot diagnose anything. Only a sleep study can diagnose a sleep condition. See our guide on [when to see a GP about snoring](/blog/when-to-see-gp-about-snoring/).

## FAQ

### Can a cold make you snore suddenly?

Yes. A cold blocks your nose, which forces air through a narrower space at speed and sets the soft tissue vibrating. This kind of snoring usually settles once the congestion clears. If it lingers long after the cold has gone, it may be worth tracking.

### Why have I started snoring with no weight gain?

Weight is only one trigger. Sudden snoring with no weight change is often down to alcohol, a new medication, sleeping on your back, allergies, or a blocked nose. Try changing one of these at a time to see which one is yours.

### Is sudden snoring a sign of sleep apnoea?

Not usually. Most new snoring has a simple, harmless cause. It is more worth checking if it comes with breathing pauses, gasping, choking, or daytime exhaustion. If that sounds like you, see your GP, who can arrange a proper assessment.
