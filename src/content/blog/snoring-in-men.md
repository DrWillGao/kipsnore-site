If your partner has nudged you in the night, you are in good company. Snoring in men is common, and there are clear reasons why men snore more than women. The good news is that most of the causes respond to fairly ordinary changes.

## Why do men snore more than women?

It comes down to anatomy and hormones. Men tend to have a longer upper airway, with more soft tissue to vibrate and collapse when the muscles relax in sleep. Men also carry fat centrally, around the neck, throat and belly, which presses on the airway. Male hormones add to it: testosterone affects airway tone and overnight breathing. Together these explain why obstructive sleep apnoea is roughly 2 to 3 times more common in men.

The main drivers, in plain terms:

| Factor | Why it matters for men |
| --- | --- |
| Neck circumference | A collar above about 43 cm (17 inches) is a recognised risk marker |
| Central fat | Men store fat around the throat and belly, narrowing the airway |
| Airway anatomy | A longer airway has more tissue to vibrate and collapse |
| Male hormones | Testosterone affects airway tone and overnight breathing control |

## Does snoring get worse for men after 40?

Often, yes. Muscle tone in the throat falls with age, so the airway relaxes more easily, and middle-age weight gain piles tissue around the neck at the same time. Our pages on [snoring and age](/blog/snoring-and-age/) and [snoring and weight](/blog/snoring-and-weight/) cover this in more detail.

## What can a man actually do about it?

Plenty, and none of it is dramatic:

- Lose a little weight if you carry it round the middle. Even a few kilograms off the neck helps.
- Cut back on alcohol in the evening, as it relaxes the throat muscles.
- Sleep on your side rather than your back.
- Stop smoking, which inflames the airway.
- Treat a blocked nose and keep a regular sleep routine.

If you also wake gasping, feel exhausted despite a full night, or your partner notices you stop breathing, please raise it with your GP. Those are the [sleep apnoea symptoms](/blog/sleep-apnoea-symptoms/) worth a conversation, and only a sleep study can diagnose it.

## FAQ

### Why do men snore more than women?

Men have a longer upper airway, a thicker neck and more fat stored centrally around the throat, all of which narrow the airway in sleep. Male hormones also affect overnight breathing control, while the hormones that protect women are largely absent. This is why snoring and sleep apnoea are more common in men.

### Is a thick neck linked to snoring in men?

Yes. More soft tissue around the neck presses on the airway when you lie down. A collar size above roughly 43 cm (17 inches) is a recognised risk marker for sleep apnoea in men. Losing a little weight around the neck and belly can ease snoring.

### Can an app tell me if I have sleep apnoea?

No. An app cannot diagnose sleep apnoea, and only a sleep study can. A tool like Kip simply tracks how loud and frequent your snoring is, so you have something concrete to show your GP, who can refer you to a sleep clinic if needed.
